The main problems in education.

Neither in school nor in college are we taught how to effectively study the material. We prepare for exams and memorize the topics. We practice solving repetitive tasks to perform better on tests, but often, it doesn't result in real understanding. Rote memorization quickly fades away and doesn't bring any benefits.

Conclusion: It is necessary to learn how to learn, but for some reason, it is not taught in many places. What can be done about it?

Specific techniques and exercises help change the approach to learning, making it effective and engaging. These same techniques are applied in the case of learning at the Practicum.

Learning techniques

Five practices from Barbara Oakley

  • Two types of attention

    Deep knowledge emerges when alternating between focused and diffuse thinking.

  • Recall

    Recalling what has been learned allows you to connect disparate pieces of memory.

  • Interleaving

    Learn multiple skills simultaneously; they enrich each other.

  • Questions

    While listening to the instructor, come up with a thought-provoking question that genuinely interests you.

  • The illusion of competence

    Repeat to yourself, write it down, and explain it to a friend: you only think you have mastered a new topic.

The story of Barbara Oakley

From childhood, Barbara struggled with mathematics. She considered herself a complete humanitarian, and not particularly intelligent at that. While in the military, she studied the Russian language to earn a bonus, and she excelled so much that she was promoted to a leadership position. However, to advance in her career, she needed to pass a mathematics exam. That's when Barbara devised her own approach to the exact sciences. It turned out that if something is "difficult" for you, the knowledge you acquire through hard work is much deeper than that of those who grasp it easily at first glance.
